Fiscal Landscape and Taxation Patterns in Connecticut (2019–2024)

Trends in Municipal Wealth: Grand List Growth

The Grand List, representing the total assessed value of taxable property in a town, is a critical indicator of local fiscal health. From 2019 to 2023, several municipalities demonstrated consistent growth in their Grand List values, reflecting economic expansion, new construction, and rising property assessments. Visualizing these trends helps identify towns with the most robust tax bases over time.

Does Population Growth Translate into Wealth Expansion?

A scatter plot comparing changes in town population with changes in Grand List values reveals mixed trends. While some towns show a positive correlation between population growth and fiscal expansion, others exhibit stagnant or declining Grand List values despite growing populations. This suggests that increased population alone does not guarantee increased taxable value, emphasizing the role of zoning, development policy, and housing markets.

Where Are Property Tax Rates Highest?

Mill rates determine the property tax paid per $1,000 of assessed value and vary widely across towns. In 2022, the highest mill rates were concentrated in urban centers and fiscally constrained municipalities. These towns often carry heavier service burdens and limited taxable property, which may drive up rates to fund essential services.

Towns with the Lowest Property Tax Burdens

Conversely, towns with the lowest mill rates in 2022 tend to be affluent suburban communities with strong Grand Lists, often supported by high-value residential and commercial real estate. These municipalities benefit from a broader tax base and require lower tax rates to meet budgetary needs, highlighting stark fiscal contrasts across the state.
